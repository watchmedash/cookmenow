Bangus Sisig

Ingredients

  • 2 large boneless bangus (milkfish) fillets, cooked and flaked
  • 1 large onion, minced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 red chili peppers, chopped (adjust to your desired spiciness)
  • 1/4 cup mayonnaise
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon calamansi juice or lemon juice
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped green onions and sliced red chili for garnish

Instructions

  1. In a large pan, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the minced onion and garlic to the pan and saut√© until the onions are translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
  3. Add the flaked bangus to the pan and cook for a few minutes until heated through.
  4. Stir in the chopped red chili peppers and continue to cook for another minute.
  5. In a small bowl, mix the mayonnaise, soy sauce, and calamansi juice (or lemon juice) to make the sisig sauce.
  6. Pour the sisig sauce over the bangus in the pan. Mix well to evenly coat the fish with the sauce.
  7. Cook for a few more minutes until the flavors meld together and the sauce slightly thickens.
  8. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  9. Transfer the Bangus Sisig to a serving plate and garnish with chopped green onions and sliced red chili.
  10. Serve hot with steamed rice or as a pulutan (appetizer) for drinks.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 15 minutes
  • Total Time: 30 minutes

Enjoy this delicious and spicy Bangus Sisig, a Filipino twist on the popular sisig dish using flavorful bangus fish!
