Chicken Pot Pie

Ingredients

  • 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breasts, cooked and cubed
  • 1/3 cup butter
  • 1/3 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried thyme
  • 1/4 teaspoon dried sage
  • 1 3/4 cups chicken broth
  • 2/3 cup milk
  • 1 cup frozen peas
  • 1 cup frozen carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen corn
  • 1 package refrigerated pie crusts (2 crusts)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. In a large saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour, salt, black pepper, thyme, and sage until well combined to form a roux.
  3. Gradually add the chicken broth and milk to the roux, stirring constantly, until the mixture thickens and comes to a boil.
  4. Reduce the heat to low, and simmer for an additional 1-2 minutes until the sauce becomes creamy.
  5. Add the cooked and cubed chicken, frozen peas, carrots, and corn to the sauce. Stir well to combine.
  6. Remove the saucepan from the heat.
  7. Unroll one pie crust and place it in the bottom of a 9-inch pie dish. Press the crust to fit the dish and trim any excess dough.
  8. Pour the chicken mixture into the pie dish over the crust.
  9. Unroll the second pie crust and place it over the filling. Press the edges of the top and bottom crusts together to seal, and flute the edges if desired.
  10. Using a sharp knife, make a few small slits on the top crust to allow steam to escape during baking.
  11. Bake the Chicken Pot Pie in the preheated oven for 30-35 minutes, or until the crust is golden brown and the filling is bubbly.
  12. Remove from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes before serving.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 20 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 30-35 minutes
  • Total Time: 50-55 minutes

Facts about Chicken Pot Pie

  • Chicken Pot Pie is a popular comfort food in Western cuisine, especially in the United States and Canada.
  • Its origins can be traced back to medieval European cuisine, where meat pies with various fillings were common.
  • Traditionally, pot pies were made with a crust on the bottom as well as the top. Nowadays, many recipes use only a top crust for easier preparation.
  • The creamy sauce in Chicken Pot Pie is typically made with a roux (butter and flour) combined with chicken broth and milk.
  • While chicken is the most common protein used in this dish, other variations may include turkey, beef, or even seafood.
  • Vegetables like peas, carrots, and corn are commonly included in the filling, but you can customize the vegetables to your preference.
  • Chicken Pot Pie is known for its rich and savory flavors, making it a comforting and hearty meal for colder months.
  • It's often served as a complete meal in one dish, making it a convenient and satisfying option for family dinners.
  • Chicken Pot Pie is perfect for using up leftover chicken or turkey from previous meals.
  • This classic dish has become a beloved staple in many households, loved by both kids and adults alike.

Enjoy the ultimate comfort food with Chicken Pot Pie, a savory pie filled with tender chicken, vegetables, and a creamy sauce, all baked to perfection under a flaky golden crust!
