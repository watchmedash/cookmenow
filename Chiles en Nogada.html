Chiles en Nogada

Ingredients

  • 6 large poblano chiles, for stuffing
  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh tomatoes
  • 1/4 cup chopped dried apricots
  • 1/4 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1/4 teaspoon cinnamon
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 4 eggs, separated
  • 1/2 cup flour
  • 1 cup pomegranate seeds
  • 1/2 cup chopped fresh parsley
  • 1 cup heavy whipping cream
  • 1 cup chopped walnuts
  • 1/2 cup sugar

Instructions

  1. Roast the poblano chiles over an open flame or under a broiler until the skin is blackened and blistered. Place them in a plastic bag, seal it and let them steam until the skin can be easily peeled off. Remove the seeds and veins, and make a small slit in the side of each chile for stuffing.
  2. In a frying pan, cook the pork, onion, and garlic until the pork is lightly browned. Add the tomatoes, apricots, parsley, and cinnamon. Cook, stirring occasionally, until most of the moisture has evaporated. Add the wine and simmer until it's reduced. Let it cool before stuffing the chiles.
  3. In a bowl, beat the egg whites to stiff peaks, then beat in the yolks one at a time. Heat oil in a large skillet. Dip the stuffed chiles in flour, shake off the excess, then dip them in the beaten egg.
  4. Fry the chiles in the oil until golden brown. Drain them on paper towels.
  5. Whip the cream until it thickens. Stir in the chopped walnuts and sugar. Spread this sauce over the chiles, garnish with pomegranate seeds and parsley.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 1 hour
  • Cooking Time: 45 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour 45 minutes

Enjoy your Chiles en Nogada!
