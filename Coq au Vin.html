Coq au Vin

Ingredients

  • 1 whole chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large onions, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 1 cup pearl onions, peeled
  • 2 cups red wine
  • 2 cups chicken stock
  • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup button mushrooms, halved

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot. Season the chicken with salt and pepper, then brown in the pot on all sides. Remove the chicken and set it aside.
  2. In the same pot, add the garlic, onions, and carrots. Cook until softened. Add the pearl onions, wine, chicken stock, thyme, rosemary, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then lower the heat to a simmer.
  3. Return the chicken to the pot, cover, and simmer for about 45 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through.
  4. In a small saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat. Stir in the flour to create a roux. Slowly whisk in a cup of the hot liquid from the pot. Once the mixture is smooth, stir it back into the pot.
  5. Add the mushrooms to the pot and cook for an additional 10 minutes, until the mushrooms are cooked and the sauce has thickened. Adjust seasonings as necessary.
  6. Serve the Coq au Vin over mashed potatoes or crusty bread.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 60 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
