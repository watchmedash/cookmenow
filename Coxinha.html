Coxinha

Ingredients

For the Dough:
  • 1 cup chicken broth
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 cup cooked and shredded chicken
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
For the Filling:
  • 1 cup cooked and shredded chicken
  • 1/2 cup cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup chopped onions
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup chopped parsley
  • Salt and pepper, to taste
For Breading and Frying:
  • 2 eggs, beaten
  • 1 cup fine breadcrumbs
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions

For the Dough:
  1. In a saucepan, bring the chicken broth, vegetable oil, and salt to a boil.
  2. Reduce the heat to low and add the all-purpose flour, stirring continuously until the dough forms a ball and pulls away from the sides of the pan.
  3. Transfer the dough to a lightly floured surface and knead it for a few minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. Let the dough cool for a few minutes before handling.
For the Filling:
  1. In a skillet, sauté the chopped onions and minced garlic until softened.
  2. Add the shredded chicken and cook until heated through.
  3. Stir in the cream cheese and chopped parsley, and season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well until the filling is creamy and well combined.
  4. Let the filling cool completely before using.
For Assembling and Frying:
  1. Take a portion of the dough and flatten it in your hand.
  2. Add a spoonful of the chicken filling in the center of the dough and shape it into a teardrop or drumstick-like form.
  3. Dip the coxinha in the beaten eggs and then roll it in fine breadcrumbs until fully coated.
  4. In a deep frying pan or pot, heat vegetable oil to 350°F (180°C).
  5. Carefully fry the coxinhas in batches until golden brown and crispy on the outside.
  6. Remove the coxinhas from the oil and place them on paper towels to drain excess oil.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 20 minutes
  • Total Time: 50 minutes

Facts about Coxinha

  • Coxinha is a popular Brazilian street food and snack.
  • The name "coxinha" means "little thigh" in Portuguese, and the snack is shaped like a chicken drumstick.
  • The dough used for coxinha is made with chicken broth, giving it a subtle chicken flavor.
  • The filling is typically made with shredded chicken, cream cheese, onions, garlic, and parsley, but there are variations with different fillings like cheese or beef.
  • After shaping and breading, coxinhas are deep-fried to achieve a crispy and golden exterior.
  • They are often served with hot sauce or a side of spicy dipping sauce.
  • Coxinha is a popular party food, especially during celebrations and gatherings in Brazil.
  • It is believed that coxinha was invented in São Paulo, Brazil, and has become a beloved snack across the country.
  • Due to its delicious taste and unique shape, coxinha has gained popularity beyond Brazil and can be found in Brazilian restaurants worldwide.

Enjoy the delightful taste of Coxinha, a savory and scrumptious treat from Brazil!
