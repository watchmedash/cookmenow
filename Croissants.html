Croissants

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • 1 packet (2 1/4 teaspoons) active dry yeast
  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1/2 cup cold milk
  • 1/4 cup cold water
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour, sugar, salt, and active dry yeast.
  2. Add the cold cubed butter to the flour mixture and use a pastry cutter or your fingers to cut the butter into the flour until you have coarse crumbs.
  3. Make a well in the center of the mixture and add the cold milk and cold water. Mix until the dough starts to come together.
  4. Transfer the dough onto a lightly floured surface and knead it for about 5 minutes until it becomes smooth and elastic.
  5. Shape the dough into a ball and place it in a lightly greased bowl. Cover with plastic wrap and let it rest in the refrigerator for at least 1 hour or overnight.
  6. After chilling, take the dough out of the refrigerator and roll it into a rectangle on a floured surface. Fold the dough into thirds (like a letter) and then roll it out again.
  7. Repeat the folding and rolling process two more times, then cover the dough and refrigerate it for 30 minutes.
  8. Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  9. Take the chilled dough out of the refrigerator and roll it into a large rectangle. Cut the dough into triangles.
  10. Starting from the wide end, roll each triangle tightly towards the tip to form a croissant shape.
  11. Place the croissants on the prepared baking sheet and brush them with the beaten egg for a shiny finish.
  12. Bake the croissants for 15-18 minutes or until they turn golden brown and flaky.
  13. Remove the croissants from the oven and let them cool on a wire rack.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 20 minutes
  • Resting Time: 1 hour (chilling) + 30 minutes (after shaping)
  • Baking Time: 15-18 minutes
  • Total Time: Approximately 2 hours (including chilling and baking)

Facts about Croissants

  • Croissants are a classic French pastry known for their buttery and flaky texture.
  • The process of making croissants involves laminating the dough with layers of butter, which creates the signature flakiness.
  • Croissants can be enjoyed plain or filled with sweet or savory fillings, such as chocolate, almond paste, or ham and cheese.
  • They are typically served for breakfast or as a snack, and are often paired with coffee or hot chocolate.
  • The word "croissant" means "crescent" in French, which describes the shape of this delightful pastry.
  • Croissants are believed to have originated in Austria and were introduced to France in the 19th century.
  • Authentic croissants are made with high-quality butter, which contributes to their rich and luxurious taste.
  • While making croissants from scratch requires time and effort, the result is well worth it, as freshly baked croissants are incredibly delicious.
  • There are many variations of croissants, including almond croissants, pain au chocolat (chocolate croissants), and ham and cheese croissants.
  • Enjoy your homemade croissants warm and flaky for the best experience!
