Ginataang Alimasag

Ingredients

  • 1 kg fresh crabs, cleaned and cut into pieces
  • 2 cups coconut milk
  • 1 cup coconut cream
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 thumbs ginger, sliced
  • 2 tablespoons shrimp paste (bagoong alamang)
  • 2-3 pieces red chili peppers (siling labuyo), chopped (optional)
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  2. Saut√© the chopped onion, minced garlic, and sliced ginger until the onion becomes translucent and the garlic is fragrant.
  3. Add the crab pieces to the pot and cook for a few minutes until they start to change color.
  4. Pour in the coconut milk and bring it to a simmer. Let the crabs cook in the coconut milk for about 10 minutes or until they are almost done.
  5. Add the shrimp paste (bagoong alamang) and chopped red chili peppers (if using) to the pot. Stir well to distribute the flavors.
  6. Pour in the coconut cream and continue to simmer for another 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the crabs are fully cooked.
  7. Season with salt and pepper according to your taste preference.
  8. Remove the pot from the heat and transfer the Ginataang Alimasag to a serving dish.
  9. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy this creamy and flavorful Filipino delicacy!

Time

  • Preparation Time: 20 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 25 minutes
  • Total Time: 45 minutes

Indulge in the rich and creamy goodness of Ginataang Alimasag, a traditional Filipino crab dish cooked in coconut milk!
