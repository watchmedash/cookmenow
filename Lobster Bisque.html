Lobster Bisque

Ingredients

  • 2 lobster tails
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 stalks of celery, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 cup white wine
  • 4 cups fish or seafood stock
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/4 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Chopped fresh chives, for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the lobster tails and boil for 8-10 minutes, or until the shells are bright red. Remove the lobster tails and let them cool. Once they are cool enough to handle, remove the meat from the shells and chop into bite-sized pieces. Set aside.
  2. In the same pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion, carrots, celery, and garlic, and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  3. De-glaze the pot with the white wine, scraping any brown bits off the bottom with a wooden spoon. Add the fish stock, bay leaf, and paprika. Bring to a simmer and let it cook for 20 minutes.
  4. Use an immersion blender or a stand blender to puree the soup until smooth. If using a stand blender, be careful when blending hot liquids and return the soup back to the pot after blending.
  5. Stir in the heavy cream and chopped lobster meat. Cook for another 5-10 minutes, or until the soup is heated through. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Divide the soup among bowls, garnish with fresh chives, and serve.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 45 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour

Enjoy your Lobster Bisque!
