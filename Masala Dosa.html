Masala Dosa

Ingredients

For the Dosa Batter:
  • 1 cup raw rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (skinned black gram)
  • 1/4 cup chana dal (split chickpeas)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Water, as needed
  • Salt, to taste
For the Potato Masala:
  • 3 large potatoes, boiled and mashed
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 green chili, finely chopped
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Curry leaves
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • Salt, to taste

Instructions

For the Dosa Batter:
  1. Wash and soak the raw rice, urad dal, chana dal, and fenugreek seeds in water for at least 5-6 hours or overnight.
  2. Drain the soaked ingredients and grind them together to form a smooth batter. Add water as needed to achieve a slightly thick pouring consistency.
  3. Add salt to the batter and mix well. Allow the batter to ferment in a warm place for about 8-10 hours or until it doubles in volume and develops a slight tangy flavor.
For the Potato Masala:
  1. Heat vegetable oil in a pan and add mustard seeds. When they start spluttering, add cumin seeds, asafoetida, and curry leaves.
  2. Add chopped onions and green chilies, and saut√© until the onions turn translucent.
  3. Add turmeric powder and mashed potatoes. Mix well and cook for a few minutes. Season with salt to taste. Remove from heat and set aside.
For Assembling:
  1. Heat a non-stick griddle or dosa pan over medium heat. Grease it lightly with oil.
  2. Pour a ladleful of dosa batter in the center of the pan and spread it outward in a circular motion to form a thin crepe.
  3. Drizzle some oil around the edges and cook the dosa until it turns golden and crispy.
  4. Place a portion of the prepared potato masala in the center of the dosa and fold it over to form a half-moon shape.
  5. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 20 minutes (excluding fermentation time for dosa batter)
  • Cooking Time: 30 minutes
  • Total Time: 50 minutes (excluding fermentation time)

Facts about Masala Dosa

  • Masala Dosa is a popular breakfast dish in South India but is loved and relished all over the country.
  • The dosa batter requires fermentation, which enhances the flavor and improves the texture of the dosa.
  • Traditionally, the dosa batter is made by grinding rice and lentils separately and then mixing them together, but ready-to-use dosa batter is also available in stores.
  • The filling for Masala Dosa, called "potato masala," is usually made with boiled and mashed potatoes mixed with spices.
  • The dosa is typically served with coconut chutney, made from grated coconut, green chilies, and spices, along with sambar, a tangy lentil and vegetable stew.
  • There are many variations of dosa, including the Mysore Masala Dosa, which has a spicy red chutney spread inside the dosa before adding the potato filling.
  • Masala Dosa is not only a beloved breakfast dish but is also enjoyed as a light meal or snack at any time of the day.
  • It is not uncommon to find Masala Dosa being served in restaurants and street food stalls across India and in Indian restaurants worldwide.
  • With its crispy texture, delightful filling, and delicious accompaniments, Masala Dosa is a delightful and fulfilling treat for dosa enthusiasts.

Enjoy the delightful flavors of South India with this delicious Masala Dosa!
