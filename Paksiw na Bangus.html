Paksiw na Bangus

Ingredients

  • 2 large bangus (milkfish), cleaned and sliced into serving pieces
  • 1/2 cup vinegar (preferably cane vinegar)
  • 1 cup water
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 thumb-sized piece of ginger, sliced
  • 1 medium-sized onion, sliced
  • 2-3 pieces long green chili peppers (siling pansigang)
  • 1 tablespoon whole peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon brown sugar
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon cooking oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. In a large pot, heat the cooking oil over medium heat.
  2. Sauté the minced garlic and sliced onions until the onions become translucent.
  3. Add the sliced ginger and sauté for a minute to release its aroma.
  4. Pour in the vinegar and water, and add the soy sauce, bay leaves, whole peppercorns, and brown sugar. Stir well to combine.
  5. Bring the mixture to a boil and then reduce the heat to a simmer.
  6. Add the sliced bangus (milkfish) to the pot, and gently stir to coat the fish with the sauce.
  7. Add the long green chili peppers to the pot. You can adjust the number of chili peppers based on your desired level of spiciness.
  8. Simmer the paksiw for about 15-20 minutes or until the fish is fully cooked and the flavors have melded together.
  9. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Remove the pot from the heat and transfer the Paksiw na Bangus to a serving dish.
  11. Serve hot with steamed rice and enjoy this tangy and comforting Filipino dish!

Time

  • Preparation Time: 15 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 20 minutes
  • Total Time: 35 minutes

Enjoy the delightful flavors of Paksiw na Bangus, a classic Filipino dish of milkfish cooked in vinegar sauce!
