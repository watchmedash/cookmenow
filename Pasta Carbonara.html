Pasta Carbonara

Ingredients

  • 12 ounces (340g) spaghetti or fettuccine
  • 4 ounces (115g) pancetta or guanciale, diced
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese
  • 1 clove garlic, minced (optional)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Salt to taste
  • Chopped fresh parsley for garnish

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pasta according to the package instructions until al dente.
  2. In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, grated Pecorino Romano cheese, minced garlic (if using), and a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper.
  3. In a large skillet, cook the diced pancetta or guanciale over medium heat until crispy and golden brown. Remove from heat and set aside.
  4. When the pasta is cooked, reserve a cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta and return it to the pot.
  5. Immediately pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, tossing quickly to coat the strands evenly. The heat from the pasta will cook the eggs and create a creamy sauce.
  6. If the sauce seems too thick, gradually add a small amount of the reserved pasta cooking water until it reaches the desired consistency.
  7. Add the cooked pancetta or guanciale to the pasta and toss again to combine.
  8. Season with salt to taste (the pancetta or guanciale is already salty, so be careful with the additional salt).
  9. Garnish the Pasta Carbonara with chopped fresh parsley and a final sprinkle of freshly ground black pepper.
  10. Serve immediately while the pasta is hot and creamy, enjoying the rich and comforting flavors of this classic Italian dish.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 10 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 15 minutes
  • Total Time: 25 minutes

Facts about Pasta Carbonara

  • Pasta Carbonara is a classic Roman dish with a debated origin, but its popularity and deliciousness are undisputed.
  • Traditional Pasta Carbonara is made with few, simple ingredients, allowing each component to shine.
  • Authentic Italian Pasta Carbonara does not include cream; the creaminess comes from the eggs and cheese emulsified with pasta cooking water.
  • The combination of pancetta or guanciale, eggs, Pecorino Romano cheese, and black pepper creates a rich, flavorful, and indulgent sauce.
  • Although there are several theories about the dish's name, some believe it was originally a meal for charcoal workers (carbonari) due to its simplicity and easy preparation.
  • While spaghetti is commonly used, other long pasta varieties like fettuccine or rigatoni can also be used in this dish.
  • Pasta Carbonara is best enjoyed immediately after preparation to savor its creamy texture and bold flavors.
  • This dish is a favorite among pasta lovers worldwide for its comforting and satisfying qualities.
  • As with many traditional recipes, there are variations of Pasta Carbonara, but the key is to keep it simple and let the flavors of the ingredients shine.
  • Prepare this authentic Italian dish at home, and you'll have a taste of Italy's culinary heritage right on your plate!

Buon appetito! Enjoy the delightful and creamy Pasta Carbonara, a true Italian classic!
