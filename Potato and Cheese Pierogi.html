Potato and Cheese Pierogi

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 large egg
  • 1/2 cup sour cream
  • 1/4 cup unsalted butter, melted
  • 1 cup mashed potatoes
  • 1 cup farmer's cheese or ricotta cheese
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped (optional)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons butter, for sautéing
  • Sour cream and chopped chives, for serving

Instructions

  1. In a large bowl, mix together the flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the egg, sour cream, and melted butter. Mix until the dough comes together.
  2. Knead the dough on a floured surface for about 5 minutes until smooth and elastic. Wrap the dough in plastic wrap and let it rest for at least 30 minutes.
  3. In a separate bowl, combine the mashed potatoes, farmer's cheese or ricotta cheese, and chopped onion (if using). Season with salt and pepper to taste. Mix well.
  4. Roll out the dough on a floured surface to about 1/8-inch thickness. Cut circles using a cookie cutter or a drinking glass.
  5. Place a small spoonful of the potato-cheese filling in the center of each dough circle. Fold the dough over the filling to form a half-moon shape. Press the edges firmly to seal.
  6. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the pierogi in batches for about 5 minutes or until they float to the surface. Remove them with a slotted spoon and drain.
  7. In a skillet, melt 2 tablespoons of butter over medium heat. Add the boiled pierogi and sauté until lightly browned on both sides.
  8. Serve the Potato and Cheese Pierogi hot with a dollop of sour cream and a sprinkle of chopped chives on top.

Time

  • Preparation Time: 45 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 15 minutes
  • Total Time: 1 hour

Facts about Pierogi

  • Pierogi is one of the most popular and beloved dishes in Polish cuisine, often considered a national dish of Poland.
  • The origin of pierogi dates back to the 13th century, and they have been a part of Polish culinary tradition for centuries.
  • Pierogi are versatile and can be filled with a variety of ingredients, such as potatoes, cheese, meat, mushrooms, sauerkraut, fruits, and even sweet fillings like jam or berries.
  • Traditional pierogi dough is made with simple ingredients like flour, egg, sour cream, and butter, resulting in a tender and slightly chewy texture.
  • The fillings and preparation methods can vary regionally in Poland and among different cultures that enjoy pierogi.
  • Pierogi are typically served as a main dish or side dish, often accompanied by sautéed onions, sour cream, or melted butter.
  • In Poland, pierogi are not only enjoyed at home but are also commonly found in restaurants, cafes, and served at special occasions and festivals.
  • Pierogi have gained popularity in many other countries and are widely appreciated as a comfort food around the world.
  • There are various ways to cook pierogi, including boiling, sautéing, frying, or even baking them in the oven.
  • During certain holidays and family gatherings in Poland, making pierogi together is a cherished tradition that brings people together to share in the joy of cooking and eating these delicious dumplings.

Enjoy the heartwarming taste of Poland with these delightful Potato and Cheese Pierogi!
