Cooking Terminologies

  • Al dente: Cooked so it's still firm when bitten.
  • Bake: To cook in an oven.
  • Baste: To moisten food during cooking with pan drippings or a special sauce to add flavor and prevent drying.
  • Beat: To mix rapidly to make a mixture smooth and light by incorporating as much air as possible.
  • Blanch: To immerse in rapidly boiling water and allow to cook slightly.
  • Blend: To combine two or more ingredients into one mixture.
  • Braise: To cook first by browning the food in butter or oil, then gently simmering in a small amount of liquid over low heat for a long period of time in a covered pot.
  • Bread: To coat with crumbs or cornmeal before cooking.
  • Broil: To cook by direct heat under a broiler or over hot coals.
  • Caramelize: To heat sugar until it liquefies and becomes a syrup.
  • Chop: To cut into small pieces.
  • Cream: The butterfat portion of milk. Also to beat ingredients, usually sugar and a fat, until smooth and fluffy.
  • Dice: To cut into very small cubes.
  • Dissolve: To cause a dry substance to pass into a solution in a liquid.
  • Dredge: To sprinkle or coat with flour or other fine substance.
  • Fold: To combine light ingredients such as whipped cream or beaten egg whites with a heavier mixture, using a gentle over-and-under motion, usually with a rubber spatula.
  • Fry: To cook in hot fat or oil.
  • Glaze: To coat with a thin, glossy mixture.
  • Grate: To rub on a grater that separates the food in various sizes of bits or shreds.
  • Grill: To cook on a grill over intense heat.
  • Knead: To work and press dough with the palms of the hands to develop the gluten in the flour.
  • Marinate: To soak in a flavored liquid; usually refers to meat.
  • Mince: To cut or chop food into extremely small pieces.
  • Mix: To combine ingredients usually by stirring.
  • Poach: To cook gently over very low heat in barely simmering liquid just to cover.
  • Puree: To mash or grind food until completely smooth.
  • Roast: To cook by dry heat in an oven.
  • Saut√©: To cook and/or brown food in a small amount of hot fat.
  • Simmer: To cook in liquid at or just below the boiling point.
  • Steam: To cook in steam in a pressure cooker, deep well cooker, double boiler, or a steamer made by fitting a rack in a kettle with a tight cover. A small amount of boiling water is used, more water being added during steaming process, if necessary.
  • Stew: To cook in liquid in a covered dish or pan, simmering or slow boiling.
  • Stir-fry: To quickly cook small pieces of food over high heat, stirring constantly.
  • Whip: To beat rapidly to incorporate air and produce expansion, as in heavy cream or egg whites.
